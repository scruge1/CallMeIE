§ 01 — Demo

How the demo works

Pick a vertical in the call. Claire qualifies you, transfers you to that vertical's receptionist, and a wrap-up SMS follows. The number is US (+1) because Irish +353 is pending IEDR and Twilio regulatory approval — the behaviour is identical.

Claire is the front-desk. She answers in an Irish accent, asks one question to qualify the call, and routes you to the right specialist AI — dental, motor factors, salon, or solicitor.

The specialist AI does the actual work: checks the calendar, quotes a price, takes a deposit, or triages pain. If anything is unclear, it escalates to the owner via SMS rather than guessing.

After the call: a wrap-up text lands on the caller's phone with the booking confirmation or next step. The whole thing takes ninety seconds.

What it handles

Six disciplines done by one system, tuned per-vertical. Not a chatbot dressed up as a phone — a receptionist that happens to be software.

I — Appointments

Calendar booking

Reads your Google Calendar in real time. Quotes genuine available slots, respects practitioner-specific diaries, and writes the event back with the caller's name, number, and reason.

II — Missed calls

Missed-call text-back

Every missed call triggers an SMS within sixty seconds — offering to book, answer a question, or send the caller back to voicemail if that is what they want. No lead goes cold.

III — Emergency

Emergency transfer

Dental pain, motor breakdown, legal urgency — the AI recognises the tone and either triages for severity or forwards straight to the owner's mobile. No "press 9 for reception" voicemail tree.

Stock & product queries

V — Hours

Opening-hours queries

Honest answers to "are you open tomorrow?" including Irish bank holidays, half-days, seasonal hours, and the annual Christmas shutdown the old voicemail never remembered to update.

VI — Report

Weekly report

Every Monday: a plain email — calls handled, bookings made, calls escalated, common questions asked. Enough signal to see whether this is earning its keep. No dashboards to log into.

§ 03 — Verticals

Four specialists, one line

Each vertical AI is trained on the language, the common questions, and the Irish-specific details that make the conversation sound right — not like a call-centre script translated to Dublin.

01Dental

Dental practices

The dental AI books new-patient exams, emergency pain slots, hygienist appointments, and follow-up checks. It knows the four Irish private insurers — VHI, Laya, Irish Life, Aviva — and can explain what each plan typically covers without quoting specific benefit figures it hasn't been shown.

It knows the PRSI Treatment Benefit: a free annual exam plus a €15 scale and polish for PAYE workers with enough contributions. It checks medical card eligibility before booking, and it triages dental pain by asking three short questions about severity and duration.

Caller
"Do ye take Laya? And how much is a filling?"
Dental AI
Yes, Bright Smile is a Laya-registered practice. A white composite filling is €110 to €160 depending on size — the dentist will quote exactly on the day. Would you like me to book you in?
02Motor Factors

Motor factors

The motor factors AI checks stock by part number or by reg + year + model. It quotes current price, confirms delivery-run availability for that afternoon, and handles account-customer queries versus walk-in pricing.

It can source parts from main-factor catalogues when a SKU isn't in local stock, note the lead time, and ring the customer back when it lands — without any human lifting the phone in between. Unusual request? It takes the reg, the customer's number, and queues an owner callback by SMS.

Caller
"Have ye a front pad set for an '18 Octavia?"
Motor AI
Two options — OEM-equivalent Brembo at €68.40, or Comline at €42.80. Both in stock. Account price? You'd be Kelly's Motors in Castletroy — €54.70 trade on the Brembo. On the 2pm run today if you confirm now.
03Salon

Hair salons

The salon AI books stylist-specific appointments — "Aoife on a Saturday" is not the same as "anyone on a Saturday". It knows service durations down to the quarter-hour (cut & blow-dry 45m, full head of highlights 2h15m, balayage 3h), and it takes a deposit on the phone for services over ninety minutes.

No-show recovery is built-in: a polite SMS at the 24-hour mark, a second at two hours, and the AI re-fills the slot from the waiting list if the confirmation doesn't come back. The stylist's day stays full without anyone chasing by hand.

Caller
"Can I get Aoife for balayage on a Saturday?"
Salon AI
Aoife has Saturday the 9th at 10:30 AM — she'll need the full three hours for balayage. €40 deposit to secure, refundable up to 24 hours. Card now or pay-link by text?
04Solicitor

Solicitors

The solicitor AI handles first-contact intake for property, family, probate, and litigation matters. It takes the caller's name, the broad nature of the matter, the other party's name if relevant, and runs a conflict check against the firm's client list before offering to book a first consultation.

If there's a conflict, it says so politely and doesn't take any further detail. If there's no conflict, it books the first-appointment slot and emails the intake form. Confidentiality-compliant by design: it never transmits case detail over SMS, only meeting-logistics messages.

Caller
"I'm thinking of separation. Does the firm do that?"
Legal AI
Yes, family law including judicial separation and divorce. I'll take your name and a contact number now for the conflict check, then book a first consultation — typically €180 for the first hour, which covers general advice and whether we can act for you.

§ 08 — FAQ

Questions we actually get

Written as they are asked. Answered honestly — including where the system is not the right fit.

01 What happens if the AI can't handle a call?

It escalates. The moment the AI detects uncertainty — an unusual request, a confused caller, an angry tone, a missing fact — it uses the transferToOwner tool: the owner's mobile rings directly with the caller still on the line, and an SMS summary of the call lands on that mobile at the same time.

If the owner doesn't pick up, the AI apologises politely and takes a callback number, not a voicemail. The owner then has everything needed to return the call in context.

02 Do you do Irish accents?

Yes. Claire speaks in an Irish accent (Leinster-neutral by default; a Munster variant is available for Limerick and Cork practices). More importantly she understands Irish accents — the AI is tested against real caller recordings from Munster, Ulster, Leinster, and Connacht.

If a specific caller is ever misheard, the transcript is logged, and the recognition model is re-tuned against that recording. This happens weekly, not once at launch.

03 Can I keep my existing number?

Yes. We don't take your number; we sit behind it. You set call forwarding on busy and no-answer (and optionally after-hours) from your existing line to a CallMeIE number — standard Irish carrier features. Your customers still ring the number printed on the van or the shopfront.

If you prefer full routing through CallMeIE, we can port the number across as part of Growth or Enterprise setup — that takes about ten working days through ComReg.

04 What happens if your server is down?

Honest answer: our server is on Render, and Render occasionally does cold-starts — typically around five seconds after a long idle period. During those five seconds, the call falls through to your mobile directly via a Twilio fallback rule. The caller never hears dead air.

In the rare case of a full Render outage, Twilio fallback keeps your existing phone ringing as if CallMeIE wasn't there. Nothing gets worse than the old behaviour; it just stops getting better for the length of the outage.

05 GDPR — how do you handle call data?

Call audio is retained for 30 days for transcription and quality purposes, then deleted. Transcripts (text only) are retained 90 days for your weekly reports and for model improvement, then deleted.

A caller can request erasure at any time — email callmeie@proton.me and we purge that caller's number and associated recordings within 72 hours. We do not sell, share, or reuse call data for anything other than running your service.

Data Processing Agreement available on request for Growth and Enterprise clients; standard processor terms included with Starter and Professional.

06 Cancellation — am I locked in?

Starter and Professional are month-to-month. Cancel any time before the next billing date by email; your setup fee is not refundable once go-live has happened, but there is no further charge.
