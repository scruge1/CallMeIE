§ 01 — Demo

How a parts call plays out

The motor factors AI reads your live catalogue, does a VRN-to-part lookup, quotes the correct price for the caller's trade account (or retail RRP), and writes the order back to your system.

§ 02 — Capabilities

What it handles for a motor factor

Six parts-counter jobs, done by one system, tuned for Irish motor-factor patterns — trade accounts, VRN lookups, delivery windows, multi-branch stock.

I — VRN lookup

Reg-to-part identification

Caller reads the Irish reg (e.g. 161-L-2345), the AI resolves it to make / model / year / engine code via RSA data, then narrows your catalogue to the correct fit — brake pads, filters, belts, whatever. No "is yours the 1.6 or the 1.8?" back-and-forth.

II — Trade accounts

Trade-vs-retail pricing

Recognises trade-account callers by Caller-ID or by asking for the account name. Quotes the correct discount tier — 15%, 20%, 25% off retail — without the trade lad having to remember their rate each call.

III — Stock check

Live multi-branch stock

Queries your inventory in real time. If out of stock at the caller's home branch, checks sister branches and quotes the transfer time. Never says "I think we have one" when the system says otherwise.

IV — Delivery windows

Van-run and courier ETA

Knows your van-run schedule — the 10am Limerick drop, the 2pm Ennis run, the next-day courier to Galway. Quotes an honest window, and if the caller misses a cut-off, tells them on the spot rather than after the order is placed.

V — Cross-reference

OEM ↔ aftermarket cross-ref

Caller reads an OEM part number — the AI returns the aftermarket equivalents in your catalogue (Febi, Meyle, Lemförder, etc.) at their price points. Good for mechanics chasing a cheaper alternative that fits.

VI — Weekly report

Monday morning summary

Plain email Monday: calls handled, orders placed, missed calls, parts asked for that you didn't stock, and the top trade accounts by call volume. Read in ninety seconds — no dashboard.

§ 03 — Depth

Built for Irish parts trade specifically

The stuff a generic "global" receptionist gets wrong every time: Irish reg plates, trade-discount tiers, van-run schedules, and the gap between what the catalogue says and what's actually on the shelf.

01Trade

Trade-account recognition

Trade callers don't want to re-introduce themselves every call. The AI recognises the account by Caller-ID for the top 80% of regulars, or confirms by asking "is that Johnny O'Brien Motors?" on the way in. Trade price quoted without the caller having to specify the discount rate.

02Retail

Retail walk-up and phone orders

Retail callers pay RRP, get asked politely for card details only via your payment terminal (never on the phone, never to the AI — GDPR / PCI clean). The AI takes the order, reserves the part, and the collector pays at the counter.

03Out-of-stock

When it's not on the shelf

The AI doesn't pretend. If the part isn't in local stock, it checks sister branches and quotes the transfer time. If it's not in any branch, it offers to order from the main distributor and quotes the realistic arrival window — the one your system says, not an optimistic guess.

§ 06 — Irish

Built for Ireland, not for "UK & Ireland"

The specifics that matter on a parts-counter call — Irish reg format, Irish trade patterns, van-run geography, regional accent handling. Generic global-AI receptionists miss all of this.

  • Irish reg platesParses both old (06-L-1234) and new (161-L-2345) formats. Knows Irish county codes L, KK, WW, CE, KY, WD, G, D, etc.
  • Van-run geographyKnows your delivery geography — the Limerick run, the Shannon run, the Ennis run, the Galway courier. Honest ETAs, not optimism.
  • Trade-account tiers15% / 20% / 25% discount tiers per account, cached from your PMS. Never leaks tier rates between accounts.
  • Irish numbersAccepts +353 mobile and landline formats interchangeably. Handles 085, 086, 087, 089 prefixes.
  • EircodeParses Eircodes for delivery addresses — good for van-run booking on courier jobs.
  • VATQuotes VAT-exclusive to trade accounts, VAT-inclusive to retail. Switchable per-call if asked.
  • Accents testedMunster, Ulster, Leinster, Connacht — tested against real caller recordings. Callers with strong regional accents aren't routed to "press 9 for English".

§ 08 — FAQ

Motor-factor questions we actually get

Written as they're asked — by branch managers and trade sales lads. Answered honestly, including where this system is not the right fit.

01 Does it integrate with my PMS / inventory system?

On Growth and Enterprise, yes — we currently integrate with the common Irish motor-factor systems: Kerridge K8 (autoparts), Catalyst, MAM Autopart, and a few others via their APIs or nightly CSV exports. On Starter / Professional the AI runs off a manually-uploaded catalogue that we refresh weekly.

If you're on a system we haven't wired yet, send us a sample export at onboarding and we'll scope the integration. Usually 2–5 working days for a new connector.

02 Will it leak trade prices to retail callers?

No. Trade prices are gated by account recognition — Caller-ID match against your trade database, or an explicit "what's the account name?" confirmation. If neither is positive, the caller is handled as retail and quoted RRP.

If a trade caller rings from an unrecognised number, they'll be asked to confirm the account name — same as a new counter hand would. No account = no trade price.

03 What if the part isn't in my catalogue?

The AI doesn't guess. If a part lookup returns nothing, it offers to take the caller's requirement and forward it to the counter — by SMS to your nominated mobile, or as a queued call-back. It never invents a part number or a price.

A weekly report lists the parts asked for that you didn't stock — useful intelligence on what to add to your range.

04 Can I keep my existing branch number?

Yes. We don't take your number; we sit behind it. Set call forwarding on busy and no-answer (and optionally after-hours) from your existing branch line to a CallMeIE number — standard carrier feature. The number on the van and the shopfront is unchanged.

Full porting is available on Growth or Enterprise: we transfer via ComReg, takes about ten working days.

05 GDPR — what's stored and for how long?

Call audio: 30 days, then deleted. Transcripts: 90 days, then deleted. Order data is retained as long as your PMS retains it — we don't add a second copy. Trade-account phone numbers are hashed for Caller-ID lookup; raw numbers are not stored outside Twilio.

A caller can request erasure — we purge within 72 hours. DPA on request for Growth / Enterprise; standard processor terms included with Starter / Professional.

06 Cancellation — am I locked in?

Starter and Professional are month-to-month. Growth has a three-month minimum because the catalogue / PMS integration is non-trivial to unwind. After that, Growth is also month-to-month. Cancel by email before the next billing date.
